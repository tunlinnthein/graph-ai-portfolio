Tan Tock Seng Hospital

Strategic Generative AI Opportunity Report & Implementation Framework

📋 Executive Summary

As a pillar of healthcare in Singapore and an anchor hospital of the National Healthcare Group (NHG), Tan Tock Seng Hospital (TTSH) is uniquely positioned to lead the region in the adoption of Generative AI (GenAI). By leveraging its multidisciplinary structure and robust staff of 8,000+, TTSH can transition from traditional digital health to intelligent healthcare. This report outlines a path to enhance patient outcomes, reduce clinician burnout, and optimize the "Mobile Inpatient Care at Home" (MIC@Home) model through strategic AI integration.

🏥 Company Overview

TTSH stands as one of Singapore's largest multidisciplinary hospitals with a legacy of pioneering medical excellence. Key operational stats include:

  • Network: Core anchor hospital for NHG Health.
  • Scale: 45 clinical and allied health departments with 16 specialist centers.
  • Human Capital: Over 8,000 dedicated healthcare professionals.
  • Innovation Focus: Strong emphasis on home-based care (MIC@Home) and community integration.

🚀 Top GenAI Opportunities

✍️

Clinical Scribe & Documentation

Implement GenAI-powered ambient listening to transcribe doctor-patient consultations directly into clinical notes, reducing administrative burden by up to 40%.

🏠

MIC@Home Virtual Assistant

Deploy specialized LLM-agents to monitor home-based patients, offering 24/7 symptom guidance and triaging urgent cases to human clinicians in real-time.

🔍

Hyper-Personalized Patient Education

Automatically generate discharge summaries and medication guides translated into multiple local languages and tailored to the patient’s specific literacy level.

📅

Operational Intelligence & Scheduling

Use generative models to predict bed demand and optimize theatre scheduling by analyzing complex clinical and historical datasets simultaneously.

📚

Institutional Knowledge RAG

Create a "TTSH Brain" using Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) to allow staff instant access to internal medical protocols, research, and governance policies.

🗺️ Implementation Roadmap

Phase 1: Foundation & Governance (Months 0-3)

Establish AI Ethics Committees and secure data privacy frameworks in alignment with MOH and PDPA guidelines. Select pilot departments (e.g., General Medicine).

Phase 2: Pilot & Proof of Concept (Months 3-9)

Launch "Clinical Scribe" and "Internal Knowledge RAG" pilots. Focus on internal efficiency before moving to patient-facing applications.

Phase 3: Integration & Expansion (Months 9-18)

Integrate successful GenAI tools into the primary EHR systems. Launch the MIC@Home virtual assistant program to support community care.

Phase 4: Full Transformation (Months 18+)

Continuous model fine-tuning based on TTSH-specific clinical outcomes. Scale GenAI across all 45 departments and 16 specialist centers.
